Erect-crested Penguin

The Crested Cliff-Dweller

The **Erect-crested Penguin** (*Eudyptes sclateri*) is a **medium-sized penguin** known for its **distinctive yellow crest** that stands upright, giving it its name. Native to the **Subantarctic islands of New Zealand**, this penguin species is one of the **most endangered** and **least known** penguins in the world. Its bold crest and striking appearance make it one of the most unique penguins.

These penguins are primarily **found on steep cliffs** and **rocky islands**, where they nest in **hidden crevices** away from predators. Their diet consists mostly of **krill, squid**, and **small fish**. Although they are **good swimmers**, they face threats from habitat loss, introduced predators, and environmental changes.

Physical Stats

Height: 55 cm (21.7 inches)

Weight: 2.5–3.5 kg (5.5–7.7 lbs)

Life Expectancy: 15–20 years

Habitat

Location: Subantarctic islands, primarily on the **Bounty Islands** and **Antipodes Islands**

Climate: Subantarctic and temperate coastal climates

Interesting Facts

  • The Erect-crested Penguin has a **distinct yellow crest** on its head, which stands upright, giving it a unique appearance.
  • These penguins are often found on **rocky islands** with **steep cliffs**, where they can nest in **hidden crevices**.
  • Their **diet mainly consists of krill**, squid, and small fish found in their subantarctic waters.
  • The Erect-crested Penguin is considered one of the **least studied** penguin species, making conservation efforts a challenge.

Conservation Efforts

The **Erect-crested Penguin** is currently listed as **Endangered** due to threats such as **habitat disturbance**, **introduced predators**, and **climate change**. With a very small population and limited breeding sites, conservation programs focus on **protecting their nesting sites** and **controlling introduced predators** on their breeding islands.

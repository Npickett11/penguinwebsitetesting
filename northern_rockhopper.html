Northern Rockhopper Penguin

The Rare Crest King

The Northern Rockhopper Penguin (*Eudyptes moseleyi*) is the **more endangered** of the two Rockhopper species. It is known for its **long, wild yellow crest feathers** and its **loud, social behavior**.

Northern Rockhoppers mainly live on **Tristan da Cunha and Gough Island**, where they breed on rocky cliffs. Their population is much smaller than their southern cousins, making them more vulnerable to extinction.

Physical Stats

Height: 50–55 cm (20–22 inches)

Weight: 2.5–4.5 kg (5.5–10 lbs)

Life Expectancy: 10–15 years

Habitat

Location: Tristan da Cunha, Gough Island

Climate: Cool, rocky coasts with strong winds

Interesting Facts

  • Northern Rockhoppers have **longer, fluffier crest feathers** than Southern Rockhoppers.
  • They are **very vocal**, using loud calls to communicate in colonies.
  • They have a **more limited range**, making them more endangered.
  • They can dive up to **100 meters (328 feet)** in search of food.

Conservation Efforts

Northern Rockhopper Penguins are classified as **Endangered**, with their numbers declining due to **climate change, oil spills, and habitat destruction**. Conservation efforts focus on **protecting their nesting grounds and reducing human impact**.

Southern Rockhopper Penguin

The Bold Cliff Jumper

The Southern Rockhopper Penguin (*Eudyptes chrysocome*) is a **small but feisty** penguin known for its **spiky yellow crest feathers** and its ability to **jump across rocky cliffs** instead of waddling.

These penguins are found on **sub-Antarctic islands** around South America, the Falkland Islands, and the Indian Ocean. They are excellent swimmers and divers, primarily feeding on krill, squid, and small fish.

Physical Stats

Height: 45–58 cm (18–23 inches)

Weight: 2–4.5 kg (4.4–10 lbs)

Life Expectancy: 10–15 years

Habitat

Location: Sub-Antarctic islands (Falklands, South America)

Climate: Rocky coastal cliffs with cold waters

Interesting Facts

  • Southern Rockhoppers **jump across rocks instead of waddling**.
  • They are **very aggressive**, often fighting with their sharp beaks.
  • They have a **distinct red eye** and a **short spiky yellow crest**.
  • They can dive up to **100 meters (328 feet)** when hunting for food.

Conservation Efforts

Southern Rockhopper Penguins are classified as **Vulnerable**, with their numbers declining due to **climate change, oil spills, and overfishing**. Conservation programs aim to **protect their breeding sites**.

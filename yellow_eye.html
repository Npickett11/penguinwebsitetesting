Yellow-eyed Penguin

The Solitary Coastal Penguin

The **Yellow-eyed Penguin** (*Megadyptes antipodes*) is a **rare and solitary** penguin native to **New Zealand**. Known for its **striking yellow eyes**, this penguin is one of the **rarest and most endangered** species of penguin in the world. Their distinct pale-yellow eye color and pale-grey plumage set them apart from other penguins.

These penguins are **largely solitary**, with each pair maintaining a **territorial nesting site**. Unlike other penguins, they are **not as social** and prefer to nest in isolated areas of the forests and coastal regions. They primarily feed on **fish**, but the increasing human activity and predators threaten their habitat.

Physical Stats

Height: 60 cm (24 inches)

Weight: 3.5–5.5 kg (7.7–12.1 lbs)

Life Expectancy: 12–20 years

Habitat

Location: Coastal regions of New Zealand, primarily on the South Island and Stewart Island

Climate: Temperate coastal and forested areas

Interesting Facts

  • Yellow-eyed Penguins have **striking yellow eyes**, which give them their name.
  • They are **largely solitary** and prefer to nest far apart from other penguins.
  • This penguin species is **one of the rarest** in the world, with only a few hundred individuals remaining.
  • They are found only in the **southernmost parts of New Zealand**.

Conservation Efforts

Yellow-eyed Penguins are **critically endangered**, facing threats from **habitat destruction**, **predation by introduced species** (like rats and stoats), and **human disturbance**. Conservation efforts are in place to protect their **nesting sites**, reduce **predator impacts**, and restore their populations through **managed breeding programs**.
